Italian Grand Prix

The Italian Grand Prix, one of the oldest and most iconic races in Formula One, has a rich history dating back to the inaugural season in 1950. Monza's Autodromo Nazionale, commonly known as Monza, has been the traditional home of the Italian Grand Prix. The circuit is celebrated for its high-speed straights and historic banked curves, making it a favorite among drivers and fans. Ferrari, the legendary Italian team, has had a significant presence at the race, and the passionate Tifosi (Ferrari fans) add to the unique atmosphere.
